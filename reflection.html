Learning Reflection

"I joined HNG thinking I was a decent backend engineer. But I know now how to build a backend that can handle real-world complexity and scale, with all the edge cases and failure modes covered."

System design

I have never been big on system design, but my time at HNG taught me how to think about the full lifecycle of a request, from the moment it hits the API to the moment it's fully processed, including all the edge cases in between. I now design with the full async flow in mind, rather than just the happy path.

Test-Driven Development

Writing tests for each core feature was a game changer for my development process. It made me think through edge cases and failure modes, and gave me the confidence to refactor without the fear of breaking anything.

Working with teams

Prior to HNG, I had only worked on solo projects. Collaborating with a team, and remotely at that, was a new experience. I learned how to communicate technical ideas clearly, and how to give and receive feedback.

Confidence

I used to have stage fright when it came to interviews, but with the number of interviews I did during HNG, I got over that fear. Now I feel confident in my ability to tackle any backend problem, and to communicate my thought process clearly in an interview setting.
